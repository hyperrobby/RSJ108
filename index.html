How to Become a Broadcast Journalist ...

Assembly time: 4 years for Bachelor degree requirement
Awesome rating: 80% awesome

Have you watched television news channels and wondered how you can be on tv one day to do the same task of informing your community on current events? It takes college experience and a bachelor's degree to get there. If I can do it then you can do it too. First, it starts with going after your dreams, putting your mind to the goals, not doubting yourself and limiting side distractions that will delay your progress time.

  • Steps to Take

  • Identify Your Interests
  • Build Your Skills
  • Pursue an College Education
  • Build an Portfolio
  • Achieve Success

  1. Find What Intersts You Daily in Life.

    This is important as people do better at jobs and tasks that involves their favorite hobby. Broadcast Journalism is great for those who love to communicate with others and use technology or visuals to inform others of current events.

  2. Build Your Skills.

    Remember, fast pace and accuracy is important to maintain high-pay jobs. The more you utilize your skills on the same daily job, the faster you become at your position and the less help you'll need. Managers will recognize your improvement and reward you.

    Eyewitness reporter live on scene.

  3. 5 Tips for Becoming a Broadcast Journalist.

    1.) It's not about being on TV if you want to be a broadcast journalist to be on air, stop. 2.) Tell a compelling story. 3.) Teamwork behind the camera. 4.) Radio is a place to start. 5.) Connect with People.

  4. Pursue an College Education.

    Full-time Journalism jobs require 4-year experience or a bachelor's degree. Whereas part-time jobs and internships require 2-year experience or a associate's degree in Journalism.

  5. Build an Portfolio.

    Having a portfolio with your accomplishments and experience listed is important to have for employers and job opportunities. Portfolios should explain concisely what your education and job qualifications are to show employers why you're a good fit for the position you've applied to.
